Hello

I'm Jonathon Keeney. I'm interested in the computational principles that evolution discovered, and whether those principles generalize to artificial intelligence.

Frontier AI needs useful abstractions across domains, not just the implementation of known techniques. Throughout my career I've been drawn to problems that are technically complex, scientifically ambiguous, or organizationally difficult to navigate. Whether studying the evolution of the human brain, developing scientific software, leading international technical standards, or exploring artificial intelligence, my work has followed the same pattern: bring structure to uncertainty, build something useful, and help others adopt it.

If you're interested in the story behind that journey, I'd be delighted to tell you more.

Selected Projects

BioCompute is an internationally recognized standard for communicating computational workflows across government, academia, and industry. The official standard has been adopted by three Centers at the FDA (CBER, CDER, and HFP), the only framework standard ever to have been adopted by the FDA's Data Standards Catalog.

  • Led cross-functional collaboration with FDA, industry, and academic partners to standardize BioCompute as IEEE 2791-2020
  • Facilitated adoption of the standard by Federal partners
  • Directed development of the BioCompute Portal
  • Delivered workshops, webinars, and technical training
ARGOS

Ideally, doctors and public health officials could send a biological sample for sequencing and know exactly what's in their sample quickly. A major barrier to this future is a high quality database that doctors and researchers can trust. A database with higher standards for quality than GenBank and more purpose-built than RefSeq. Argos is a regulatory-grade database of infectious disease genomes that can do exactly that. As a Co-Investigator for the Argos project, I co-led development of the regulatory decision-support platform that integrates heterogeneous infectious disease datasets for scientific and government users.

  • Translated scientific requirements into practical platform capabilities
  • Designed curation and analytical workflows, QA processes, and visualizations
  • Presented platform capabilities to scientific and government stakeholders
  • Deployed the software in regulated environments with support from dev ops

The High-performance Integrated Virtual Environment (HIVE) is a bioinformatics platform originally developed at the FDA for both scientific research and regulatory submissions. The George Washington University runs the only publicly accessible instance of this software, which is used for internal research, and for collaborations with Federal partners, other institutions, and private industry. In addition to research, I led platform operations, authored and enforced policy, carried out user support, training, and technical consulting for other scientists.

  • Led or supported research projects using bioinformatic workflows, QA/QC, and machine learning
  • Supported users across regulated scientific environments
  • Worked with software engineers and FDA DevOps teams
  • Developed training, documentation, and onboarding resources
