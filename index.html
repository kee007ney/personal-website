Hello!

I'm Jonathon Keeney. I enjoy exploring complex systems, asking difficult questions, and building practical solutions that help people make sense of them. Over the years, that curiosity has taken me from studying the genetics of human brain evolution to developing scientific software, international technical standards, AI-enabled workflows, and new ways of thinking about science education. This website is a collection of those projects, the ideas behind them, and the people and communities that have shaped the journey.

Big questions are rarely answered all at once. They become answerable by asking better questions. Throughout my career, I've been drawn to problems that are technically complex, scientifically ambiguous, or organizationally difficult to navigate. Whether developing international technical standards, building scientific software platforms, studying the evolution of the human brain, or exploring how artificial intelligence can transform scientific workflows, my role has consistently been the same: bring structure to uncertainty.

I enjoy translating broad ideas into concrete questions, building practical solutions—whether those take the form of software, data models, analytical methods, educational programs, or technical standards—and helping organizations adopt them successfully.

Much of my scientific work has focused on the genetics of human brain development and evolution, particularly through comparative studies of living and extinct primates. More broadly, I'm fascinated by systems: how they emerge, how they evolve, and how they can be understood through computation, data, and careful experimentation.

Alongside my research, I've worked extensively with federal agencies, academic institutions, and industry partners to develop technologies, establish standards, teach new methods, and build communities around emerging ideas. Outside the laboratory, those same interests have led me to educational outreach, international volunteer work, and the design of interactive systems that help people understand complex subjects through exploration.

Selected Projects

BioCompute

International standard for communicating complex bioinformatics workflows.

BioCompute is an internationally recognized standard for communicating computational workflows across government, academia, and industry. The official standard has been adopted by three Centers at the FDA (CBER, CDER, and HFP), the only framework standard ever to have been adopted by the FDA's Data Standards Catalog.

  • Led cross-functional collaboration with FDA, industry, and academic partners to standardize BioCompute as IEEE 2791-2020
  • Facilitated adoption of the standard by Federal partners
  • Directed development of the BioCompute Portal
  • Delivered workshops, webinars, and technical training

Argos

Government-facing infectious disease knowledge platform for FDA and BARDA.

Co-led development of a regulatory decision-support platform integrating heterogeneous infectious disease datasets for scientific and government users.

  • Translated scientific requirements into practical platform capabilities.
  • Designed analytical workflows, QA processes, and visualizations.
  • Presented platform capabilities to scientific and government stakeholders.

HIVE Bioinformatics Platform

Bioinformatics platform supporting scientific users in regulated environments.

The High-performance Integrated Virtual Environment (HIVE) is a bioinformatics platform originally developed at the FDA for both scientific research and regulatory submissions. The George Washington University runs the only publicly accessible instance of this software, which is used for internal research, and for collaborations with Federal partners, other institutions, and private industry. In addition to research, I led platform operations, authored and enforced policy, carried out user support, training, and technical consulting for other scientists.

  • Led or supported research projects using bioinformatic workflows, QA/QC, and machine learning
  • Supported users across regulated scientific environments.
  • Worked with software engineers and FDA DevOps teams.
  • Developed training, documentation, and onboarding resources.
